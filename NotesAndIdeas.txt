	Gakh Nazg -ûr the Golog goth -lata the kil,
	Three Rings for the Elven-kings under the sky,

	udu -ûr the Shakutarbik goth ulub hajat -ob gund,
	Seven for the Dwarf-lords in their halls of stone,

	krith -ûr matuurz najor duump -u mat,
	Nine for Mortal Men doomed to die,

	ash -ûr the burz goth -mab tab burz soli
	One for the Dark Lord on his dark throne

	-ishi the uzg -ob mordor amal the burguul reg.
	In the Land of Mordor where the Shadows lie.

	ash Nazg -ûr -u durb tak gith, ash Nazg -u gimb tak,
	One Ring to rule them all, One Ring to find them,

	ash Nazg -u thrak tak gith, and -ishi the burzum doturog tak,
	One Ring to bring them all, and in the darkness bind them,

	-ishi the uzg of mordor amal the burguul reg.
	In the Land of Mordor where the Shadows lie.
	
	
	Nazg gakh uzg gith tak. Nazg. U.

	Hajat. ulub najor duump -mab tab burz gith, gith ash gith -u nazg.

+ A quick plaigerism search of the first chapter of this text suggests it is pulled from:
	- A book on the 'anthropology of consciousness' (http://www.scribd.com/doc/61247081/Nature-of-Magic)
	- An introduction to the New Testament of the Bible (http://www.scribd.com/doc/97670112/Introduction-to-the-New-Testament-Vol-1-1909-Theodor-Zahn)
	- A novel titled "The Color of His Blood" (http://www.scribd.com/doc/56046861/The-Color-of-His-Blood)
	- The classic "Wind in the Willows" (http://www.scribd.com/doc/138521616/The-Wind-in-the-Willows)
